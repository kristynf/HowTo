How to Embed Formalin-Fixed/Paraffin Infiltrated Tissue


Basic Embedding Steps

  1. Gather the cassettes to be embedded and place them into holding compartment of the embedding center.
  2. Ensure you have enough forceps and molds that are warming to complete the task at hand.
  3. Ensure that the paraffin reservoir has enough melted paraffin
  4. Only have one cassette open at a time to avoid swapping samples
  5. Open a cassette and observe the size of the tissue. Retrieve and appropriately sized mold, ensuring you leave a few mm of space of paraffin around the sample
  6. Dispense a small amount of paraffin into the mold and place the tissue, cut or inked surface down.
  7. Use your forceps or a tamper to ensure that your tissue is flat against the mold surface and move it over to the heat sink or cold plate to begin the process of paraffin solidification
  8. Once your tissue is firmly in place, Place the cassette lid on top of the mold, this will serve as the ID, and dispense more paraffin on top to fill the top of the cassette.
  9. Move the cassette over to the cold plate
  10. Repeat this process until all your cassettes are embedded. A normal histotech should be able to embed at a rate of 60-70 cassettes an hour at a minimum.
  11. Once the cassettes have completely solidified, they can be removed from the basemold and trimmed of excess paraffin. They are then ready for microtomy.
