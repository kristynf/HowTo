Microtomy Steps in a Nutshell

  1. Gather needed supplies: ice tray, microtome blades, paraffin blocks, brushes, personal protective equipment, microscope slides, forceps
  2. Ensure that the water bath temperature is sufficient for the type of paraffin used (usually around 42 degrees Celsius)
  3. Trim the blocks you will be cutting and place face down on ice
  4. Ensure the blocks are sufficiently chilled for the tissue type you are sectioning
  5. Section the block at the correct thickness for the tissue type and the lab's protocol (typically between 2 and 5 micrometers)
  6. While sectioning, avoid artifacts such as knife strikes and folds in the ribbon
  7. Place the ribbon you obtain carefully on the waterbath, again trying to avoid artifacts such as folding
  8. Inspect the ribbon and select the best section
  9. If there are no good sections, try again, otherwise careful pick up a section using a microscope slide
  10. Place the microscope slide on a rack to air dry, it will then be ready for subsequent staining steps
